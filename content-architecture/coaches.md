## Hub Definition

- **Working title:** Complete guide to social/content/discoverability for coaches and consultants
- **Folder slug:** `coaches/`
- **Primary keyword:** `social media marketing for coaches`
- **Primary intent:** Informational (secondary: commercial investigation — coaches and consultants evaluating repeatable content systems)
- **Target audience:** Solo coaches, consultants, executive coaches, business coaches, career coaches, and small expertise-led advisory businesses that sell trust, judgment, and client outcomes rather than physical products or local inventory.
- **Core problem solved:** A single authoritative resource for coaches and consultants trying to turn expertise into discoverable content across social platforms, LinkedIn, Google, and AI-search surfaces without drifting into generic creator growth, generic small-business marketing, or product documentation.
- **Hub mission:** Own the AI-era discoverability playbook for solo expertise businesses: how to package lived expertise into content that builds authority, attracts qualified clients, supports referrals, and funnels naturally to Launchpad as the execution layer.
- **Audience proof points:** The radar found no existing `_posts/coaches/` folder and no meaningful coach/consultant GSC baseline, making this a greenfield vertical build. Exact coach-social head terms are modest (`social media marketing for coaches` at 140/mo LOW), but adjacent authority and acquisition terms are stronger (`thought leadership content` at 720/mo LOW and `how to get coaching clients` at 480/mo LOW). Competitor coverage is active, so the defensible wedge is positioning discipline, not topic absence.
- **Opening framing:** Frame the reader's problem as a trust bottleneck: coaches and consultants are not trying to become generic influencers; they need a practical system for proving expertise, staying visible, and earning qualified conversations without sounding like interchangeable AI content.
- **Canonical angle:** Expertise-business first. The cluster applies social media, SEO, GEO, LinkedIn, AI tools, and content planning to the specific job of building personal authority and winning clients. It funnels to Launchpad, but it is not a product cluster.

### Validation

- Can stand alone as a flagship page targeting `social media marketing for coaches`, with secondary support from `marketing for consultants`, `marketing for coaching business`, `coach marketing`, `thought leadership content`, and `content marketing for coaches`.
- Not a listicle — synthesizes platform choice, thought leadership, client acquisition, SEO, GEO, LinkedIn, AI workflow, and calendar discipline into one vertical strategy.
- Risk: pressure to become a generic "marketing for coaches" encyclopedia. Hub must signpost to spokes and stay anchored to discoverability and content systems, not broad sales tactics, pricing, offers, or coaching-business operations.

## Risk Notes

### Cannibalization risk: LOW within cluster, MEDIUM cross-cluster

**Internal to the cluster:**

- **C1 (Thought Leadership) vs C4 (LinkedIn):** Both discuss authority. Mitigation: C1 owns the platform-agnostic authority framework; C4 owns LinkedIn-specific application for coaches and consultants.
- **C2 (Client Acquisition) vs C7 (Content Ideas + Calendar):** Both can mention funnel stages. Mitigation: C2 owns how content earns qualified conversations; C7 owns what to post and when. C7 should avoid teaching a full acquisition strategy.
- **C3 (SEO) vs C6 (GEO):** Both are discoverability topics. Mitigation: C3 is Google, local, service-page, and personal-brand SEO; C6 is AI recommendation and LLM citation/application. Each intro must explicitly distinguish the surfaces.
- **C5 (AI Tools) vs C7 (Calendar):** Both can mention batching. Mitigation: C5 owns tool/workflow evaluation; C7 owns the in-page 30-day content plan.

**Cross-cluster:**

- **Volume asymmetry vs Local Businesses:** Coach head terms are modest (`social media marketing for coaches` 140/mo, `linkedin for coaches` 50/mo, `GEO for coaches` 0/mo). A future Local Businesses cluster may show broader local-volume upside. Tradeoff: Coaches is still viable because it has low competition, greenfield repo coverage, strong authority/acquisition adjacent terms, and a clear Launchpad funnel fit. Do not judge this cluster only against Local Businesses by total search volume.
- **Boundary risk vs `content-creators/*`:** Content Creators owns creator growth, distribution, monetization, and generic content pillars. Coaches owns service-business client acquisition, authority, booked calls, referrals, and expertise proof. The cluster should not optimize for follower growth as the primary success metric.
- **Boundary risk vs `small-business/` and Local Businesses:** Small Business owns cross-vertical SMB constraints and Local Businesses owns storefront/local-service breadth. Coaches may include local SEO, but through an expertise/personal-brand lens: credentials, service pages, reviews, city/service positioning, and individual practitioner trust. Do not route this cluster under `small-business/`.
- **C4 vs `social-media-platforms/linkedin/`:** LinkedIn cluster owns mechanics, formats, timing, scheduling, and analytics. C4 owns how coaches and consultants use LinkedIn to prove expertise and start qualified conversations.
- **C6 vs `discoverability/geo/`:** GEO cluster owns theory and general tactics. C6 applies GEO to coaches and consultants. It links to GEO for definitions, then pivots immediately to coaching-specific trust and authority signals.
- **C7 vs `content-planning/content-calendar/`:** Content Calendar cluster owns generic calendar theory and templates. C7 must include the confirmed structured 30-day in-page calendar table for coaches, not a downloadable, and not a generic calendar explainer.
- **Coaches vs `brandghost/launchpad/`:** Coaches funnels to Launchpad but is not a product cluster. C5 may mention Launchpad as an execution path, but no Phase 1 article should become a pricing page, feature page, or `Launchpad for coaches` BOFU article.

### Ambiguous intent: NONE

All Phase 1 pages have a single clear primary intent. MOFU pages carry commercial-investigation framing because the reader is evaluating how to win clients or choose a workflow, but each page still owns one narrow problem.

### Pages that may need future splitting

- **C2 (Client acquisition)** may eventually split into "life coaching clients", "business coaching clients", "career coaching clients", and "consulting clients" if subvertical demand validates.
- **C4 (LinkedIn)** may split into executive-coach, career-coach, and consultant LinkedIn spokes if LinkedIn traffic converts.
- **C5 (AI tools)** may split into "ChatGPT for coaches", "AI coaching-session tools", and "AI content tools for coaches" if search intent proves distinct enough.
- **C7 (Content ideas + calendar)** may split into separate idea bank and calendar-template spokes if the combined page grows too broad or if `content calendar for coaches` begins showing measurable demand.

### Deferred to Phase 2 (NOT Phase 1)

- Best social media platforms for coaches — exact demand is 0, so cover platform choice inside the hub and use C4 as the LinkedIn depth article.
- Instagram for life coaches / Instagram for coaches — exact demand is lower or subvertical-specific; defer until LinkedIn and content-calendar performance are known.
- Health coach, life coach, career coach, executive coach, and consultant-specific marketing spokes.
- AEO / voice search for coaches — vertical exact demand is 0; revisit after C6 validates AI-search interest or after Local Businesses shows stronger local-voice demand.
- `Launchpad for coaches` BOFU page — defer to Launchpad Phase 2 or a product cluster expansion after this vertical proves conversion value.
- Local SEO depth for storefront-style businesses — belongs to Local Businesses, not Coaches, unless framed tightly around individual practitioners.
