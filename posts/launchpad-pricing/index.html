BrandGhost Launchpad Pricing: Plans, Credits, and What You Get

BrandGhost pricing for Launchpad is designed around content output, publishing needs, and credits. If you are close to choosing a plan, the important question is not just “What does it cost?” It is “Which plan gives me the workflow I need without paying for capacity I will not use?”

The official BrandGhost pricing page lists a Free tier, Starter at $250 per month, Growth starting at $500 per month, and custom Pro pricing for Launchpad. It also lists a 100-credit top-up pack for $20 and says credits never expire: BrandGhost pricing.

This guide breaks down what each plan includes, how credits fit into the workflow, and which plan is most likely to match your use case.

BrandGhost Pricing at a Glance

The official pricing page is the source of truth for current details, but the Launchpad pricing structure can be understood in four tiers: Free, Starter, Growth, and Pro.

Plan Pricing source Best fit
Free Listed on the BrandGhost pricing page Testing the workflow and reviewing limited output.
Starter Listed on the BrandGhost pricing page Small teams that want a monthly content plan, blog posts, social posts, and auto-publishing.
Growth Listed on the BrandGhost pricing page Teams that need more monthly blog and social output.
Pro Listed as custom pricing on the BrandGhost pricing page Teams that need a custom plan or larger rollout.

The plans are not only separated by price. They differ by output volume, publishing support, and available extras. That matters because Launchpad is not simply a writing tool. It is a workflow for turning brand context into a content plan, blog content, and social content.

What the Free Plan Includes

The Free tier is for evaluation. BrandGhost’s pricing page lists Free as including 30 copy-paste social posts and 5 blog posts: BrandGhost pricing.

That makes Free useful when you want to see the shape of the output before committing to a paid workflow. It can help you answer practical questions:

  1. Does the content reflect the brand well enough to review?
  2. Are the blog ideas relevant to the audience?
  3. Do the social posts feel useful across channels?
  4. How much editing would your team need before publishing?
  5. Does the workflow save enough time to justify a paid plan?

The Free tier should not be evaluated as a full ongoing content operation. It is better viewed as a test drive. If the output is directionally useful and your team wants monthly planning, auto-publishing, and credits for extras, the next question is whether Starter or Growth fits the expected volume.

Starter Plan: Who It Fits

Starter is listed at $250 per month on the BrandGhost pricing page. The page describes Starter as including auto-publish to 2 social accounts, 30 social posts per month, 5 blog posts per month, a new content plan every month, content that sounds like you, and 50 monthly credits for extras: BrandGhost pricing.

Starter is likely the most practical plan for a small team that wants a consistent monthly workflow but does not need high volume. It gives the team enough output to maintain a basic rhythm: blog content for depth and social content for distribution.

Starter is a strong fit when:

Use case Why Starter fits
Solo founder Enough content to move from sporadic posting to a monthly plan.
Small business Blog and social output without hiring a full content team.
Lean marketer A structured workflow for strategy, blog, and social assets.
Early agency test A way to evaluate Launchpad for one brand or limited client use.

The auto-publish support is also important. Copy-paste output can be useful, but publishing friction often causes content to stall. Starter reduces that friction for teams that want the workflow to reach live channels more easily.

Growth Plan: When More Volume Makes Sense

Growth starts at $500 per month according to the official pricing page. BrandGhost lists Growth as including 90 social posts per month and 15 blog posts per month: BrandGhost pricing.

Growth is for teams that have enough review capacity to use more output. That point matters. More content is useful only if the team can edit, approve, publish, and learn from it. If extra drafts sit unused, the plan is not creating value.

Growth may fit when:

  1. You manage multiple active channels.
  2. You need several blog assets per month.
  3. You have a reviewer who can approve content consistently.
  4. You want to test more topics, angles, or campaigns.
  5. You already know that Starter volume would be too limiting.

The decision between Starter and Growth should be based on realistic publishing capacity, not ambition alone. If your team can only review one blog post per week, Starter may be enough. If you have a steady review process and need a higher cadence, Growth gives more room.

Pro Plan and Custom Needs

The Pro plan is listed as custom pricing on the BrandGhost pricing page: BrandGhost pricing. Custom pricing usually makes sense when the standard tiers do not map cleanly to the team’s needs.

Pro may be relevant for agencies, larger teams, or organizations that need a custom arrangement. For example, a team might need more output, different support, or a rollout that does not fit Starter or Growth.

Because Pro is custom, the right next step is to define requirements before starting a pricing conversation. Be ready to answer:

Requirement Why it matters
Number of brands or accounts Affects workflow complexity.
Monthly blog volume Determines content production needs.
Monthly social volume Determines distribution capacity.
Review process Shows how content will be approved.
Publishing needs Clarifies whether automation matters.

That preparation helps avoid buying a custom plan before the workflow is clear.

How Launchpad Credits Work

BrandGhost’s pricing page says top-up credits can be used for rewrites, extra posts, calendar refreshes, and more. It lists a 100-credit pack at $20 and says credits never expire: BrandGhost pricing.

Credits are useful because content workflows rarely fit a perfect monthly pattern. One month you may need extra rewrites. Another month you may want additional posts for a campaign. Another month you may refresh the calendar after a positioning update.

The key is to treat credits as flexible capacity, not as a reason to skip planning. If you regularly need a large number of extras, the current plan may be too small. If you only need extras occasionally, top-ups can be a cleaner solution than upgrading immediately.

Use credits for work that extends or improves the plan:

  1. Rewriting a draft that needs a different angle.
  2. Creating extra posts around a timely campaign.
  3. Refreshing a calendar after strategic changes.
  4. Testing alternative content versions.
  5. Filling temporary gaps without changing plans.

Because the BrandGhost pricing page says credits never expire, they can support occasional bursts without the pressure to use them immediately.

Choosing the Right BrandGhost Pricing Tier

The right BrandGhost pricing tier depends on output volume, review capacity, and publishing maturity.

Choose Free if you are still validating the workflow. Your goal is to inspect the output, understand the editing lift, and decide whether Launchpad fits the way your team thinks about content.

Choose Starter if you want a consistent monthly content workflow with manageable volume. Starter is often the practical choice for small teams that need blog and social content but do not have a large review operation.

Choose Growth if your team has enough publishing demand and review capacity to use higher output. Growth can support a stronger cadence, but it works best when the team is ready to approve and distribute content reliably.

Choose Pro if your requirements are outside the standard tiers. This is the path for custom needs, larger operations, or cases where the normal plan structure does not fit.

Here is the decision in plain language:

Situation Suggested plan to evaluate
“I want to see what Launchpad produces.” Free
“I need a monthly content rhythm.” Starter
“I need more blog and social volume.” Growth
“I have custom scale or support needs.” Pro

Pricing Mistakes to Avoid

The most common pricing mistake is choosing based only on the monthly number. A content workflow plan should be judged by the usable output it creates.

Avoid choosing Free and expecting it to behave like an ongoing content operation. It is best for evaluation.

Avoid choosing Starter if you already know the included output will not cover the channels and cadence you need. Paying less but constantly needing extras may not be the cleanest workflow.

Avoid choosing Growth if your team cannot review the additional output. More drafts without review capacity can create backlog instead of momentum.

Avoid choosing Pro before documenting what makes your needs custom. A clearer requirement list leads to a better pricing conversation.

BrandGhost pricing makes the most sense when matched to the job Launchpad is supposed to do: turn brand context into content strategy, blog content, and social content at a pace your team can sustain. Start with the smallest plan that can prove the workflow, then expand when review capacity and publishing demand justify it.

How to Budget for BrandGhost Pricing Over Time

BrandGhost pricing is easier to evaluate when you separate the subscription cost from the team’s operating capacity. The plan should match the amount of content the team can realistically review, publish, and learn from.

Start with the review budget. Even if a plan includes more output, someone still needs to approve topics, check claims, refine voice, and decide what goes live. If review capacity is limited, a smaller plan may create more value than a larger plan that produces unused drafts.

Next, consider publishing channels. A team using only one or two social accounts may not need the same volume as a team managing several active channels. Blog cadence matters too. More blog posts are useful only when the team has the strategy and review process to support them.

Then plan for variable months. Some months may require rewrites, extra posts, or calendar refreshes. Credits can help with that flexibility, especially when the need is occasional rather than constant. If extras become routine, it may be a sign that the base plan should be revisited.

Finally, compare the plan to the cost of doing the same work manually. That does not mean every minute saved becomes profit. It means the team should understand which tasks Launchpad is replacing or accelerating: strategy setup, draft creation, social adaptation, calendar preparation, or recurring content refreshes.

The right BrandGhost pricing tier should make the workflow easier to sustain. If the plan creates more review pressure than the team can handle, it is too much. If the team consistently runs out of capacity and uses credits every cycle, it may be too small.

BrandGhost Pricing Review Questions

Before choosing a plan, write down the work you expect Launchpad to support each month. How many blog posts can your team review? How many social accounts need content? How often will you refresh the strategy? How much editing is realistic before publishing?

Those questions make BrandGhost pricing easier to compare because they connect the plan to behavior. A plan is valuable when the included output turns into reviewed, useful, published content. It is less valuable when the team buys capacity but does not have the process to use it.

If you are unsure, start with the smallest plan that proves the workflow. Then upgrade when the review process, publishing cadence, and need for extra capacity are clear.
